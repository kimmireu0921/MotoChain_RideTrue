The Problem

Two markets broken by the same root cause

No trustless, tamper-proof record of what actually happened — to the bike, or to the rider.

🏍️

Secondhand Motorcycle Market

Odometers are tampered, accident records concealed, and titles manipulated. Buyers on Naver Cafe or Bungae Jangter have no way to verify a seller's claims — costing money and creating real safety risks.

📋

Motorcycle Insurance in Korea

Premiums are set by age and registration year — blunt proxies with no connection to actual behavior. Safe young riders who leave a family plan face premiums that nearly double overnight, despite zero change in real risk.

Protocol 1

MotoChain — Motorcycle Identity & History Registry

Each bike receives a permanent on-chain identity token anchored to its VIN. Every subsequent event — ownership change, service, accident — is cryptographically signed and publicly auditable.

Incentive Design

Mechanic Incentive Loop

The registry is only as trustworthy as the mechanics who write to it. The incentive model must make fraud unprofitable — not just penalized when caught.

🏆

Tiered Reputation Staking

Mechanics begin with a small stake and unlock higher write-access tiers — and higher fees — as reputation grows. A top-tier mechanic has far more to lose. Fraud becomes exponentially more costly as success increases.

⏳

Escrow Window

Fees are held in a 60-day escrow after submission. If a dispute or inconsistency is flagged within this window, the fee is clawed back along with a stake penalty. The reward is only real if the record holds.

🔍

Cross-Validator Challenges

Any verified mechanic or inspector can challenge a submitted record within the escrow window. A successful challenge earns a share of the slashed stake — creating community policing without a central authority.

✅ Honest Mechanic

Fee released after 60 days. Reputation score increases. Unlocks higher tier, higher fees per entry, and a growing protocol credential visible to all customers and platforms.

❌ Fraudulent Entry

Fee clawed back. Stake slashed — the higher the tier, the larger the slash. Tier downgraded. Record flagged on-chain permanently. Challenger earns a reward from the slashed stake.

Why staking isn't a burden: Mechanics already pay large upfront affiliation fees to brands like Honda to access legitimacy and customers. Protocol staking replaces that cost with a credential that is publicly auditable, portable across platforms, and earns back returns through honest participation. The key difference: Honda fees are sunk costs. Protocol stakes grow in value with reputation.

Portable Reputation — Eliminating the Blank-Slate Penalty

Today, a young rider who spent years under a family plan gets no credit for that safe riding history when they register independently. RideTrue's on-chain behavioral record is self-owned and fully portable — it follows the rider across insurers and platforms permanently, eliminating the arbitrary premium cliff.
